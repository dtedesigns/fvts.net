Did a Virgin Really Have a Baby?

The virgin birth of Jesus of Nazareth is at the very root of our Christmas traditions. Needless to say many people wink, even scoff, at such a story and dismiss it as myth. As they argue, virgins just don’t have babies. No thinking person can possibly believe it. The common popular account of the origin of the Christmas story, then, is that it is a fictitious tale, borrowed in its most fundamental elements from the ubiquitous pagan cults that populated the ancient world. In short, it’s been borrowed and baptized into the Christian tradition. Others, however, take this story as literal history. It really happened; God performed a miracle. Well, did it happen? Is this the work of God? Or is it a story by men? How can we know? How can we find out? Where did the story come from if it’s not an historical account? And perhaps most importantly, what does it mean if it is a true story? Is it simply a charming tale we tell during a brief season in winter to help lift our holiday sentiment? Or is there something more lasting, more profound here? Location and Time September 11, 2010
2:00-4:00 p.m.
Batavia Public Library
