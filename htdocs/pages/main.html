Is Religion Dangerous?

Would Life Be Better Without Religion?

The Fox Valley Theological Society is sponsoring a public lecture and discussion forum on the topic of violence in the name of faith. Join the conversation as we together think about the philosophical, theological and practical implications of religious fanaticism.

Saturday, December 4 at 2:00 pm
Batavia Public Library
10 S. Batavia Avenue
Batavia, Illinois 60510 Map

Our Speaker

Nicholas Piotrowski

Nicholas is a resident of Batavia and a PhD candidate at Wheaton College. His dissertation addresses first century socio-rhetorical uses of sacred texts for the sake of community formation. However, this upcoming talk and discussion will revolve around a very different topic.

In 1996 the American Humanist Society awarded Richard Dawkins with the “Humanist of the Year Award.” He opened his acceptance speech with these words: “It is fashionable to wax apocalyptic about the threat to humanity posed by the AIDS virus, ‘mad cow’ disease, and many others, but I think a case can be made that faith is one of the world’s great evils, comparable to the smallpox virus but harder to eradicate.” Since hearing these words, Nicholas has thought deeply about why someone would believe this, and whether or not it’s a fair statement. Before moving to the Fox Valley Nicholas ministered to juvenile offenders in Colorado, Mississippi, and then Washington DC. The boys he worked with came directly from (and participated in the formation of) quite violent communities. During these years of ministry he lived directly in the matrix of violence and religion. He also taught, among other things, History and Ethics at the Washington Christian Academy in Olney, Maryland. There he was able to flesh out and develop many of his burgeoning ideas on this topic. On September 11th he’ll briefly share some of his thoughts before opening the floor to question and dialogue.
