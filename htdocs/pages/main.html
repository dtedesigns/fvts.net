Can A Good God Allow Suffering And Evil?

C. S. Lewis and the Problem of Evil & Suffering

The Fox Valley Theological Society is sponsoring a public lecture and discussion forum on the topic of suffering and evil. Join the conversation as we together think diligently about the philosophical, theological and practical implications of the very real presence of suffering and evil in our world.

Saturday, May 29 at 10:00 am
Batavia Public Library
10 S. Batavia Avenue
Batavia, Illinois 60510 Map

Our Speaker

Dr. Jerry Root (Ph.D., British Open University)
Adjunct Professor of Christian Formation & Ministry, Wheaton College (IL)

Dr. Root is an expert on C.S. Lewis and has found pain and suffering to be a thread throughout much of his writings. More...

Jerry was born and raised in Southern California. He grew up in a Christian family but did not become a Christian until his freshman year at Whittier College.

He was deeply influenced by the ministries of Campus Crusade for Christ and Granada Heights Friends Church in La Mirada, California. Jerry's sister Kathy introduced him to the writings of C. S. Lewis while he was still an undergraduate. Upon graduation from college he selected Lewis as an author who would take him to other authors and has made of him a life study. The concept that graduation from college is "Commencement" has meant that only a foundation for learning is established through formal education after which one commences his/her liberal arts education.

He pastored three different churches over twenty-three years. Nineteen of those years were invested in student ministry, and for four years he served as a senior pastor. While pastoring he taught courses in Philosophy and on C. S. Lewis for ten years at the College of Du Page in Glen Ellyn, Illinois. He has served on the Adjunct Faculty at Biola University since 1991, teaching courses on Lewis. He has been teaching at Wheaton College since 1996. Jerry has lectured or preached in 14 different countries around the world and has also lectured extensively throughout the United States.
