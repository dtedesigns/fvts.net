How Did Humans Emerge From The Mud?

Did we evolve, or were we created? Whether we are just another animal or created in the image of God has important personal and societal implications. Do we have to choose between science and religion, or is there a way to reconcile the two? This issue tends to spark heated debate, but our conversation will try to generate light rather than heat.

Our Speaker

Gerald Rau (Ph.D. Cornell), Independent Scholar,
Chiayi Taiwan

Dr. Rau has spent his career teaching Biology to high school and college students in both America and Taiwan. After fifteen years teaching high school and middle school science at Lincoln American School in Taiwan, Dr. Rau recently taught Biology at Wheaton College and Trinity Christian College before returning to Taiwan. His academic passions include the intersection of science and faith and how the fundamentals of learning and education come to bear upon this subject. Dr. Rau is currently writing a book, to be published by Inter-Varsity Press, which explores these important topics. Dr. Rau was educated at Wesleyan University (B.A. biology), the University of Minnesota (M.A. horticulture), and Cornell University (Ph.D. plant breeding), and returned to the University of Michigan a few years ago for an M.A. in science education, concentrating on the philosophy of science in relation to science teaching.
